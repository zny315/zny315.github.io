About

Researcher in training at the intersection of fire safety and materials engineering.

I am a graduate student at the State Key Laboratory of Fire Safety, University of Science and Technology of China, advised by Prof. Yuan Hu. My current work focuses on fire-safe polymer systems and material strategies for reducing heat release, smoke production, and combustion hazards.

Before graduate study, I trained in Safety Science and Engineering at USTC, with a minor in Computer Science and Technology. That background shaped my interest in connecting experiments, fire modeling, and materials design.

Research Focus

Fire-safe polymer and cable materials

01

Flame-Retardant Materials

Designing polymer composite systems that improve char formation, thermal stability, and flame-retardant performance.

02

Smoke Suppression

Studying strategies that reduce smoke generation and toxic hazard during combustion of cable-related materials.

03

Fire Modeling

Using engineering tools and simulation workflows to connect material behavior with fire safety scenarios.
